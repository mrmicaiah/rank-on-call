## Purpose

This document turns the funnel decision in the master doc into an actual build list: which pages get written, in what order, targeting what.

**The governing principle: Rank On Call sells a $39 diagnosis, not a website.** So every piece of content answers one question — *"is MY site broken? what's wrong with **mine**, specifically?"* — and ends in the Deep Dive CTA. That is the **DIAGNOSTIC lane**.

The sister site Sites On Call already owns the **TEACHING lane** — *"how does local SEO work / how do I fix it"* — with ~22 published articles. **Rank On Call must NOT re-teach what SOC teaches.** It diagnoses, then hands off: cross-link to SOC's teaching article for the "how to fix," and to sitesoncall.com for "just fix it for me."

Same symptom as SOC. Different verb.

> **No city pages.** Not one. See the warning in `PROJECT_MASTER.md` → *The Funnel*. If a future session finds itself scaffolding location templates in this repo, it has imported the wrong playbook from the sister site.

### The voice

Every article here gets written in Sites On Call's house voice, aimed at a contractor and nobody else.

- **Honest-broker energy.** "The honest version." "No BS." "What they don't tell you." Actively tell people when they *don't* need something — that's what makes the rest believable.
- **Credibility by contrast.** SOC earns it with lines like *"written by people who've never pulled a P-trap."* Rank On Call's version is **diagnostic** credibility: *"most audits are a sales pitch in a trench coat. Here's what a real one finds."*
- **Contractor language, never marketer language.** Say *get the phone ringing*, *showing up on Google*, *not getting calls*. **Never** say *traffic*, *impressions*, or *funnel*.

  This isn't a style preference, it's measured: **"why is my website not showing up on google" = 590/mo**, while the marketer phrasing **"why is my website not getting traffic" = 10/mo**. A **60x** difference in the same question. Write how contractors talk or the content doesn't get found at all.
- **Money loss made visceral.** *Light it on fire. Money pit. Renting your customers.*

### On the 0/0 phrasings — read this before anyone "fixes" the plan

A large family of obvious-sounding phrasings returns **0 exact-match volume**: *"why can't I find my business on google," "why is my contractor website not getting leads," "contractor not getting enough work,"* and the entire *"phone isn't ringing"* family.

Zero volume here does **not** mean zero demand. These are **diffuse clusters** — the same question asked a hundred slightly different ways, each phrasing individually too small to measure, collectively large. The demand is real; it's just spread too thin for any one string to register.

**The method that follows from that:** target the **measurable anchor** with a comprehensive hub article, and let the cluster get caught by writing thoroughly around it — the variants surface as long-tail against a page that covers the whole question.

**Do NOT create separate pages for 0/0 variants.** A page per phrasing produces a pile of thin, near-duplicate articles competing with each other for a search that was never measurable in the first place.

## The three diagnostic hubs

Three pillar pages, each with a cluster of diagnostic articles feeding it. The cluster articles link **up** to their pillar; the pillar links **down** to them.

Every article idea below leans on what the Deep Dive actually finds — the real findings from the Rob cold test in the master doc (NAP inconsistency across site/Yelp/BBB, broken stat counters, brand-identity mismatch, ranking position in own city, domain-name problems, thin GBP). That's deliberate: **the content is a preview of the product.** Each article names a specific defect the report catches, so the CTA is a promise the deliverable already keeps.

## The CTA structure

Every article — pillar or cluster, all three hubs — ends in the same two-part move. Write it once, follow it everywhere.

**Part 1 — name the reader's likely specific problem.** Not a generic sign-off. The last paragraph reflects back the actual defect that article is about: *"If you've got two phone numbers floating around out there, you're not losing calls — you're never hearing them ring."* The reader should feel identified before they're sold anything.

**Part 2 — the Deep Dive CTA, verbatim shape:**

> **Find out exactly what's wrong with your online presence — get your Deep Dive for $39** (normally $249 — founding price through 2026).

*(Price, anchor, and offer window are rendered from `src/_data/offer.json` via the CTA include — this template describes the shape, the data file defines the values.)*

**Then the SOC cross-link, as the alternate door:**

> Want someone to just fix all this? → **sitesoncall.com**

And where a relevant SOC teaching article exists, link it inline as the "here's how this works" companion — see the cross-link column in each hub table.

**Why both doors, always:** the reader who wants to know goes to the Deep Dive; the reader who wants it handled goes to Sites On Call. Neither is a lost visitor, and neither site cannibalizes the other. Two doors, one funnel.

## Build priority

**Pillar pages come before their cluster articles.** The cluster links up to the pillar — writing clusters first leaves them pointing at nothing and forces a re-link pass later.

**Start with Hub 1 (Visibility).** It has the easiest and highest-volume anchors in the pyramid: `contractor SEO` at **720/mo and EASY** (18/100 competition — the softest target in the whole data set) plus a **590/mo** pain anchor. Best opportunity-to-effort ratio available.

Recommended order:

1. **Hub 1 pillar** — *"Why Your Contractor Website Isn't Showing Up on Google"*
2. **1.1** — *"You Rank #4 in Your Own City"* (highest pain, most immediate self-recognition)
3. **1.2** — *"Your Google Business Profile Is Half-Built"*
4. **1.6** — *"Most Contractors Are Losing Google to Middlemen"* (activates the flagship SOC bridge early)
5. **Hub 2 pillar** — the emotional core, highest CPC
6. **Hub 3 pillar** + **3.1** *"The 3-Addresses Problem"* (the report's centerpiece finding)
7. Remaining cluster articles, hub by hub

Rationale for leading with Hub 1: it is the cheapest ranking win, and it feeds the other two — a contractor who arrives asking *"why can't people find me"* is one click from *"why isn't my phone ringing"* and *"what's actually broken on my site."*
